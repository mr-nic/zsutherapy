Keeping Clients and Growing by Referral

0 / 0
Rebooking — The Single Biggest Lever
At the End of Every Session
“Shall we get your next one in the diary?” — ask before they leave
Suggest a specific interval based on what you noticed in the session
Book it there and then — not “I’ll message you”

Rebooking in the room converts far higher than following up later

If They Are Not Ready
Send a warm follow-up 3–4 weeks later
Keep a simple note of their preferred treatment and usual frequency
“Hope you’re still feeling the benefit — let me know when you’d like to come in again”
Referrals — Word of Mouth, Made Easy
Make Referring Feel Natural
Mention it after a session where a client is clearly pleased
“If anyone you know is looking for this kind of work, I’d love to look after them”
If someone sends a friend, make sure the new client is warmly received
If you want to offer something in return, keep it simple and personal: a small credit toward their next session

Word of mouth is already the top channel — this just makes it a little more intentional

Thank Every Referrer
A personal message when someone sends a friend means a lot
Apply any credit quietly, without making it feel transactional
Reactivation — Clients Who Have Drifted
Spot Them Monthly
Once a month, note who has not been in for 3 or more months
Prioritise people who used to come regularly
Reach Out Gently
“Hi [name] — it’s been a while and I’ve been thinking of you. I have some slots coming up if you’ve been meaning to come in.”
Keep it short, warm, and without pressure
A small gesture — a priority slot or a quiet mention of something new you’re offering — can tip the decision

A past client already trusts you — the bar to return is much lower than for a new client

Loyalty — Recognising Regular Clients
Simple Ways to Show Appreciation
Remember what matters to them — their job, their problem areas, how they prefer to work
A birthday message or a seasonal check-in costs nothing and means a lot
For regular clients, a block booking option — a few sessions paid upfront at a small saving — rewards loyalty without feeling like a points card
Keep any scheme simple enough to track without effort

Personal continuity is the thing a sole practitioner can offer that a clinic or spa cannot

Reviews — Let Happy Clients Speak for You
Ask at the Right Moment
Ask just after a session they clearly enjoyed — not by automated message
“A Google review would genuinely help me — here’s the link if you have a moment”
Save your direct Google review link on your phone
Aim for 2 new reviews a month — regularity matters more than volume
Use Reviews Well
Reply to every review warmly and specifically — never with a copy-pasted response
Share standout reviews on Instagram Stories as proof, not as a hard sell
Add a Google review link to the website footer and About page

Reviews support local search visibility and reassure first-time clients

What Makes Zsu Different
Make This Visible
15 years of practice — say it on the website, the bio, and in conversation
Five modalities under one roof — few sole practitioners offer this range
Pregnancy massage as a specialism — limited local competition, high reassurance value
“One therapist, always Zsu” — continuity is a genuine differentiator

These are not marketing claims — they are true and worth saying clearly

Where to Say It
Website About page — lead with experience and specialisms
Instagram bio — one quiet line that says it without shouting
Pin a “who I am and why this matters” post on Instagram
Let it come through in how you communicate, not just what you publish
