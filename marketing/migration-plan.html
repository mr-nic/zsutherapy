Wix to Self-Hosted — Ownership and Flexibility Plan

0 / 0
Why Migrate — The Real Goal
This Is About Control, Not Just Cost
Reduced platform lock-in — own your site, not just rent it
Portability — move to any platform later without a redesign
Lower monthly cost is a benefit, but not the main reason
Potential annual saving: approximately £800–900 (treat as approximate, not guaranteed)

Only migrate if the timing is right — a rushed migration causes more problems than it solves

When It Makes Sense to Migrate
When the new site is fully built and tested — never before
When you have time to do it carefully across 2–3 weekends
When booking, SEO, and email continuity are the top priorities
If Wix starts limiting your growth or the cost becomes harder to justify
Phase 0 — Before You Touch Anything
Backup Everything First
Copy all text content from every page — all 12 pages
Download all images from Wix Media Manager
Export your email subscriber list as CSV
Screenshot your current Wix DNS settings
Write down every URL slug exactly as they appear
Note your Wix renewal date — do not cancel until the new site is live

Do not skip this phase — it is the safety net for everything that follows

Phase 1 — Hosting and Email
Hosting: Hostinger (recommended starting point)
Sign up at hostinger.com — Business Shared Hosting (~£2.99/month)
Do not transfer your domain yet — get hosting working first
Install WordPress via the one-click installer
Test on the temporary URL before touching anything live
Email: Zoho Mail (free) or Google Workspace (£5.20/month)
Zoho: sign up at zoho.com/mail, add zsutherapy.com, create zsu@zsutherapy.com
Google Workspace: familiar Gmail interface with custom domain
Set up and test email before transferring the domain
Confirm deliverability with a test send before going live

Email migration carries real risk — test thoroughly before transferring DNS

Phase 2 — Build the WordPress Site
Theme and Builder
Theme: Astra or Kadence (free, fast, well supported)
Page builder: Elementor free — closest feel to Wix drag-and-drop
Essential Plugins (all free)
Yoast SEO — page titles, meta descriptions, sitemap
Redirection — preserves all 12 existing URLs (do not skip this)
WP Forms Lite — contact form
UpdraftPlus — automatic backups
Recreate All 12 Pages

/pregnancy-massage · /reflexology · /thai-yoga-massage
/gift-vouchers · /book-online · /hours-locations · /about · /faq

Set up URL redirects in the Redirection plugin before going live — this protects Google rankings

Phase 3 — Bookings
Option A: Fresha (recommended)
Free account at fresha.com — purpose-built for therapists
Set up all treatments, durations, and prices
Embed the booking widget on /book-online
Update Google Business Profile booking link
Update Instagram bio link
Test the full booking flow from a phone before promoting

Booking continuity is more important than speed — test every step

Option B: Simply Schedule Appointments (WordPress plugin)
Free basic version handles simple appointment booking
Keeps everything within WordPress
More setup required than Fresha
Phase 4 — Domain Transfer and Launch
Domain Transfer (do last)
Unlock domain in Wix: Domains > Manage > Transfer Away
Get the EPP/Auth code from Wix
Initiate transfer at Hostinger or move DNS to Cloudflare (free, recommended)
Wait 24–48 hours for propagation — plan for brief downtime
Do this on a quiet day with low bookings
Launch Readiness Checklist
All 12 pages live and tested on the temporary URL
All URL redirects set up and tested
Email working on new setup
Booking flow tested end-to-end from a phone
Google Business Profile updated with new booking link if changed
Fallback plan agreed: if migration stalls, Wix stays live until it is resolved

Do not cancel Wix until every dependency above is confirmed working
